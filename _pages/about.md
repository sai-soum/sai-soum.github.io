I am doctoral reasercher at the [Centre for Digital Music ](https://www.c4dm.eecs.qmul.ac.uk/), [Queen Mary University of London](https://www.qmul.ac.uk/) under the [AI and Music Centre for Doctoral Training Program](https://www.aim.qmul.ac.uk/). My research focuses on design of user-centric context-aware AI-based tools for music production. As a hobbyist musician and producer myself, I am interested in developing tools that can support creativity and collaboration resulting in emergence and novelty. I am also interested in the broader implications of AI in music and the arts, and how it can be used to democratize music production and consumption. My research is supported by the [UKRI](https://epsrc.ukri.org/) and [Steinberg Media Technologies GmbH](https://www.steinberg.net/). My research approach is user-centric, incorporating expert knowledge and involving prototyping and extensive user evaluations. I am also a student member of the [Audio Engineering Society](https://www.aes.org/). I am currently on the organising committee of the [AES AI and ML for Audio Conference 2025](https://aes2.org/events-calendar/2025-aes-international-conference-on-artificial-intelligence-and-machine-learning-for-audio/), serving as a speical session chair. 
Furthermore, I find my daily dose of calm in practicing yoga, crocheting garments, and [painting outdoors](https://www.saisoumya.com/art). I also volunteer regularly at [Foodcycle](https://foodcycle.org.uk/) and enjoy travelling, exploring new cultures, analog film photography, fibre arts, and art history, specifically impressionism and secession.  
You can check my artistic [here](https://www.saisoumya.com/). I am currently porting my website to Jekyll, so please bear with me.
